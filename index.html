Affective Visual Dialog : A Large-Scale Benchmark for Developing Emotion-Aware Vision Learners

Abstract

The ability of AI systems to understand human feelings enables them to be more compatible with our emotional being, informing higher quality interactions with us and hence better service towards our goals. In this paper, we study the role of affective language in the form of conversation grounded on visual stimuli in informing human decisions about his/her emotion. Toward this goal, we collected a new dataset, dubbed AffectVisDial, of 30800 dialogs of 10-turns as well as concluding emotion attributions and dialog-informed textual explanations, resulting in a total of 15781 hours working hours. The dialog starts with an image and two emotion opinions about the image (one positive and one negative). Then two participants, a Questioner and an Answerer , are instructed to have a conversation through which the Questioner ( can not see the image) asks questions to the Answerer for the purpose of deciding how he/she feels about it. In the end, we asked both the Questioner and Answerer to provide the emotion they felt and an explanation of what in the conversation informed their decision. Building on AffectVisDial, we comprehensively study the Affective Visual Dialog task through two skills: (1) Dialog-based Question Answering: the skill of generating the last answer in the conversation or at an arbitrary turn given the dialog history and the image ( similar to the typical visual dialog setting). We also introduce (2) Dialog-based Emotion classification and Affective explanation: the skill of predicting the emotion class and corresponding affective explanation based on the dialog history for the Questioner or the dialog history combined and the image from the Answerer. We train and demonstrate strong Affective Visual Dialog baselines adapted from state-of-the-art models. Remarkably, the responses generated by our models how promising emotional reasoning abilities in response to visually grounded conversations. The dataset and code will be made publicly available, and we hope that will help ease future affective vision research and that Affective Visual Dialog serves as a test for machine emotional intelligence.
